How Dental Chews Work

Dental chews work through mechanical abrasion — as your dog chews, the texture scrapes plaque off the teeth, similar to how brushing works for humans. The best chews also contain active ingredients like sodium hexametaphosphate or zinc sulfate that chemically inhibit tartar formation. Some include chlorophyll or parsley for breath freshening.

The key quality indicator is the VOHC (Veterinary Oral Health Council) seal. Products with this seal have been independently tested and proven to reduce plaque or tartar by at least 10-20%. Not all dental chews carry this seal, so it's an important differentiator.

What Makes a Great Dental Chew

Four factors matter most: Efficacy — does it actually reduce plaque? Look for the VOHC seal. Ingredients — avoid artificial colors, BHA/BHT preservatives, and excessive sugar. Size appropriateness — too small and it's a choking hazard; too large and small dogs can't chew effectively. Digestibility — the chew should break down safely in the stomach.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I give my dog dental chews?
Most dental chews are designed for daily use. Check the packaging for your dogs size and weight recommendations. One chew per day is the standard for maintaining oral health between professional cleanings.
Are dental chews a substitute for brushing?
No, dental chews complement brushing but dont replace it. Think of them like mouthwash for dogs — helpful, but not a complete oral care solution. Veterinary dental cleanings are still recommended annually.
Can puppies have dental chews?
Most dental chews are formulated for adult dogs (6+ months). Puppies have different dental needs and softer teeth. Look for puppy-specific dental treats, and always supervise chewing sessions.
What does the VOHC seal mean?
VOHC stands for Veterinary Oral Health Council. Products with this seal have been tested and proven to reduce plaque or tartar. Its the closest thing to a vet stamp of approval for dental products.
Can dental chews cause choking?
Any chew can pose a choking risk if your dog tries to swallow large pieces. Always choose the correct size for your dog, supervise chewing, and remove small remnants. Avoid giving chews to aggressive gulpers without supervision.
