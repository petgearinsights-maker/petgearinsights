Why Hydration Matters So Much for Cats

Cats evolved as desert animals, getting most of their moisture from prey. Domestic cats on dry food diets often exist in a state of mild chronic dehydration, which strains the kidneys over time. Chronic kidney disease (CKD) affects up to 30-40% of cats over age 10. A water fountain that encourages regular drinking is one of the simplest preventive health measures you can take.

Stainless Steel vs. Plastic vs. Ceramic

Stainless steel wins on hygiene, durability, and ease of cleaning. It's dishwasher-safe, doesn't scratch, and resists biofilm. Ceramic is also non-porous and attractive, but it's heavy, breakable, and more expensive. Plastic is cheapest but scratches easily, harboring bacteria in micro-grooves, and some cats develop chin acne from plastic contact. For long-term value and health, stainless steel is the clear winner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why stainless steel over plastic?
Stainless steel is non-porous, resisting bacterial buildup that plagues plastic fountains. It doesnt scratch easily, preventing biofilm formation in grooves. Its also free from BPA and other chemicals that can leach from plastic into your cats water.
How often should I clean a cat water fountain?
Deep clean weekly by disassembling all parts and scrubbing with a bottle brush. Replace the filter every 2-4 weeks depending on usage. Rinse the pump monthly to prevent mineral buildup.
Do cats prefer running water?
Yes, most cats instinctively prefer moving water because in nature, flowing water is typically safer than stagnant pools. A fountain can encourage cats who are reluctant drinkers to consume more water, reducing the risk of kidney and urinary issues.
Are stainless steel fountains noisy?
Quality stainless steel fountains are very quiet — typically under 40 dB, comparable to a whisper. Noise usually indicates low water level or a pump that needs cleaning. Most cats are unbothered by the gentle gurgling sound.
How much water should a cat drink daily?
Cats need approximately 3.5 to 4.5 ounces of water per 5 pounds of body weight daily. A 10-pound cat should drink about 7-9 ounces per day. Cats on wet food diets may drink less since wet food contains roughly 80% water.
