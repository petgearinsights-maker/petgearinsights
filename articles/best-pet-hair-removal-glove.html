If you have a pet, you have fur — on your couch, your clothes, your car seats, and somehow inside your morning coffee. Lint rollers work but burn through refills fast. Rubber gloves and electrostatic gloves offer a reusable, more effective alternative. How Hair Removal Gloves Work

There are two main types. Electrostatic gloves use a special fabric that creates a static charge when rubbed across surfaces, attracting loose hair. They work well on clothing and upholstery. Textured rubber/silicone gloves use raised nubs or ridges that physically lift hair from fabrics through friction. Some gloves combine both approaches. The key advantage over lint rollers: they're reusable, create zero waste, and often work better on embedded fur.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do pet hair removal gloves work on all fabrics?
They work best on tightly woven fabrics like couch upholstery, cotton, and polyester. Performance decreases on loose weaves, knits, and delicate fabrics. For delicates, use a gentle sticky option rather than aggressive rubbing.
How do I clean the glove after use?
For electrostatic gloves, simply shake or brush off collected fur, or rinse under water. Silicone grooming gloves can be rinsed and dried. Most are maintenance-free and ready to use again immediately.
Are these gloves good for people with pet allergies?
They can help reduce allergens in your home by removing fur and dander from furniture and clothing. However, they are not a substitute for air purifiers, regular vacuuming with HEPA filters, and other allergy management strategies.
Can I use hair removal gloves on my pets directly?
Only grooming-specific gloves like the DELOMO are designed for direct pet contact. Electrostatic gloves are for furniture and clothing only. Using the wrong type on your pet could cause discomfort or static shocks.
Do they work in the car?
Yes, hair removal gloves work very well on car seat upholstery. Keep one in your glove compartment for quick cleanups before passengers ride. They are especially effective on cloth seats.
