Every year, an estimated 100,000 dogs are injured in car accidents in the US alone. Unrestrained dogs can become projectiles in a sudden stop, endangering both themselves and human passengers. For small dogs under 25 pounds, a proper car seat isn't a luxury — it's a safety essential. These elevated booster seats keep small dogs secure, comfortable, and at window height for the ride. We evaluated the top options for stability, crash-test performance, ease of installation, and comfort.

Why Small Dogs Need Car Seats

Small dogs face unique risks in vehicles. A 10-pound unrestrained dog in a 30 mph crash generates roughly 300 pounds of force — enough to injure the dog severely and anyone it hits. Beyond safety, car seats reduce motion sickness by stabilizing your dog and providing a consistent visual horizon. They also prevent distracting behavior like climbing onto the driver's lap.

Key Features to Evaluate

Crash-test certification: Look for seats tested to automotive safety standards (CPS certification). Attachment system: ISOFIX/LATCH compatibility is most secure. Tether system: Must include a short leash attached to a harness (never a collar). Washability: Removable, machine-washable covers are essential. Size: Should accommodate your dog sitting and lying down comfortably.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a dog car seat in the front seat?
It is safest to use dog car seats in the back seat to avoid airbag deployment risks. If you must use the front seat, disable the passenger airbag and use the furthest-back position setting.
Do I still need a harness with a car seat?
Yes. A car seat elevates and contains your dog, but a crash-rated harness with a short tether prevents ejection during sudden stops. Never attach the tether to a collar — always use a harness.
What size car seat for a 15-pound dog?
Most small dog car seats accommodate dogs up to 20-30 pounds. A 15-pound dog should be comfortable in any standard small car seat. Measure your dog sitting upright and compare to the seats internal dimensions.
Are dog car seats required by law?
Laws vary by state and country. Several US states require pets to be restrained while driving. Regardless of local laws, an unrestrained dog is a safety hazard during sudden stops or accidents.
How do I get my dog used to a car seat?
Start by placing the car seat in your home and letting your dog explore it with treats and praise. Then install it in the car for short, positive trips. Most dogs adjust within 3-5 car rides.
